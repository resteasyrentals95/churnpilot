Rules & Data

The issuer rules the plan is built on, plus the point valuations used everywhere in the app — you can edit them.

Chase 5/24 rule
Chase will not approve most of their cards if you've opened 5 or more personal cards from ANY bank in the past 24 months. This is why every good churning plan starts with Chase. Business cards from Chase, Amex, Citi, BofA, and Wells Fargo do not count toward 5/24 — but Chase business cards still require you to be under 5/24 to get approved.
Amex rules — once per lifetime & pacing
Amex generally gives each welcome bonus once per lifetime per card (roughly a 7-year memory in practice). You can hold up to 5 Amex credit cards, and can be approved for about 2 cards per 90 days. Always check for the "lifetime language" pop-up before applying — if no pop-up appears, you're eligible.
Capital One pacing
Capital One is stingiest with approvals: roughly 1 card every 6 months, and they pull all 3 credit bureaus. Their business cards DO report to your personal credit report (unlike most issuers), so they can add to 5/24.
Citi rules
Citi allows 1 card per 8 days and max 2 per 65 days. Many Citi bonuses have 48-month rules — you can earn the same bonus again 48 months after you last earned it. Citi business cards don't report to personal credit.
Sapphire 48-month rule
You can only earn a Sapphire (Preferred or Reserve) bonus once every 48 months, and can only hold one Sapphire card at a time.
Golden rules of churning (read this!)
1) Never carry a balance — interest wipes out every point you earn. Pay in full monthly.
2) Never spend money you wouldn't have spent anyway just to hit a bonus.
3) Hit the minimum spend with real expenses: business costs, taxes, insurance, groceries, rent (via Bilt).
4) Space applications out — this app's plan does that for you.
5) Keep no-fee cards open forever (helps credit age); downgrade annual-fee cards you don't use instead of canceling.
